Chapel Camera → Zoom Setup

Step-by-step instructions to connect the chapel PTZ camera to Zoom using NDI Tools.

Last updated: 2026-01-23
Quick checklist
  • ✅ Laptop (Mac/PC) + power
  • ✅ Connected to chapel network (Ethernet preferred)
  • ✅ NDI Tools installed
  • ✅ Zoom signed into the Church account
  • ✅ Verify audio + video are both NDI in Zoom
What you need
  • Laptop (Mac or PC)
  • Access to the Church Zoom account
  • Optional: a personal Zoom account on a phone to confirm what viewers see
  • NDI Tools installed (from ndi.video/tools)
Network guidance
  • Ethernet is best (most reliable).
  • If Wi-Fi: connect to the chapel-approved network for streaming.
  • Avoid overcrowded networks when possible (stream stability matters).
(Passwords rotate—keep them in a private document.)

Setup steps

  1. Open NDI Tools.
  2. Click Video Monitor.

  1. In Video Monitor, choose the UHD Camera then choose your camera to see what the camera sees.
  2. You should see the chapel video stream appear.
PTZ presets
Use presets for common views (speaker close-up, full stand, etc.).

  1. In NDI Tools, open Virtual Input (sometimes called NDI Webcam / NDI Virtual Input).
  2. Select the same camera feed so it becomes your “webcam” source.

Pick video source
  1. Open Zoom and join the meeting.
  2. For Camera, select NDI Virtual Camera.
Pick audio source (important)
  1. For Microphone, select NDI Audio.
  2. For Speaker, also select NDI Audio if that's your setup.
  3. Avoid using the laptop mic—people may hear room noise near the laptop.
Rule of thumb
If viewers say “video is fine but audio is wrong” (or vice-versa), it usually means Zoom isn’t using the NDI devices. Go back and re-select NDI Virtual Camera and NDI Audio.

  1. Join the meeting from a phone (personal Zoom) to verify the stream looks right.
  2. If needed, copy the invite link and share it to the right audience.
Audio tip for music
If music sounds "thin" or gets heavily processed, check Zoom's Original sound for musicians setting (on/off depending on the use case).

Troubleshooting

I can't see the chapel camera in NDI
  • Confirm you're on the chapel network (Ethernet preferred).
  • Restart NDI Tools and open Video Monitor again.
  • Try a different network port / cable if available.
Zoom doesn't show NDI camera/audio
  • Make sure Virtual Input is running and has the correct feed selected.
  • Quit Zoom fully and re-open it (device lists can "stick").
  • In Zoom, re-select NDI Virtual Camera and NDI Audio.
Fast reset (works surprisingly often)
  1. Leave Zoom meeting.
  2. Close Zoom.
  3. Stop Virtual Input.
  4. Open Video Monitor → re-select camera feed.
  5. Start Virtual Input → re-select camera feed.
  6. Open Zoom → join meeting → select NDI video + NDI audio.
